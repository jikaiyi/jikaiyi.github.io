About Me

I am now a postdoctoral research fellow at the Electrical Engineering and Computer Science Department of the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor, working with Prof. Lei Ying. I received my doctoral degree from the Electrical and Computer Engineering Department of The Ohio State University in December, 2021, under the supervision of Prof. Yingbin Liang. I was a visiting student research collaborator at the department of Electrical Engineering, Princeton University working with Prof. H. Vincent Poor. Previously I obtained my B.S. degree from University of Science and Technology of China in 2016.

Research

I have been working at the intersection of optimization, machine learning and complex systems, on both the theory and application sides. My major research focuses include:

  • Bilevel deep learning (theory, algorithm, and application in autoML, communication networks, database systems, etc.)

  • Meta-learning (optimization theory and scalable algorithmic design)

  • Large-scale optimization (theory and algorithm for big-data applications)

  • Machine learning for communication and caching networks
